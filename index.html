Explaining My Growth

At the beginning of the semester, I focused mostly on making designs look interesting. Now I understand that strong design needs structure, hierarchy, balance, and purpose. These projects show how I improved both creatively and technically.

Food & Wine Gala Poster (April 2026)

Software: Adobe Illustrator

Description: A professional event poster using illustration, typography, and composition.

Learning: This project changed how I think about design. I learned how to guide the viewer’s eye, control composition, and balance multiple elements without making the design feel crowded. It also helped me improve my typography and understand how text should work with visuals.

Reflection: Strong composition and color. Some areas are slightly busy.

Dreamscape Portal (2026)

Software: Adobe Photoshop

Description: A surreal composition with an astronaut and glowing portal.

Learning: I learned how to create atmosphere and depth using lighting and blending. This project helped me understand storytelling in design.

Reflection: Strong mood and concept. Needs smoother blending.

Face Vector Trace (2026)

Software: Adobe Illustrator

Description: A stylized vector portrait using simplified shapes.

Learning: I learned how to simplify details and focus on key shapes. This improved my observation and precision.

Reflection: Clean and strong. Needs smoother curves.

JLC Logo (2026)

Software: Adobe Illustrator

Description: A personal logo design representing my identity.

Learning: I learned that logos must be simple, balanced, and readable at all sizes.

Reflection: Good concept. Needs testing at smaller sizes.

Final Class Project (May 2026)

Software: Adobe Photoshop / Illustrator

Description: This project represents the final application of everything I learned this semester.

Learning: This project helped me bring together composition, typography, and structure. I focused more on making intentional decisions instead of guessing.

Reflection: Shows my growth as a designer and my ability to think more professionally.

Digital Artist Point of View

This semester changed how I see design. Before, I focused on making things look good, but now I understand design is about structure, balance, and communication.

Illustrator taught me precision and discipline, while Photoshop helped me explore creativity and mood. Now I think more intentionally and pay attention to details like spacing and typography.

I also started noticing design everywhere in daily life, analyzing layouts, colors, and structure automatically. This shows how much I’ve grown.

Overall, I feel more confident. I’m not just creating anymore — I’m thinking like a designer.
